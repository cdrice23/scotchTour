Famous Scotch Whisky Brands

Cardhu

Region: Speyside

Founded: 1824

Interesting Fact: The word "cardhu" derives from the Scots Gaelic Carn Dubh, meaning "Black Rock".

Dalwhinnie

Region: Highland

Founded: 1898

Interesting Fact: Dalwhinnie has the highest-elevation working distillery in Scotland, at 1164 feet above sea level.

Erdradour

Region: Highland

Founded: 1825

Interesting Fact: Producing only 12 casks of Scotch whisky a week, Erdradour Distillery is the smallest traditional distillery in Scotland.

Glenfarclas

Region: Speyside

Founded: 1805

Interesting Fact: Glenfarclas was one of the first distilleries in Scotland to open a visitor center, which it opened to the public in 1973.

Glenmorangie

Region: Highland

Founded: 1843

Interesting Fact: Glenmorangie uses the tallest stills of any distillery producing Scotch, at 8 meters tall (26'3").

Glenlivet

Region: Speyside

Founded: 1824

Interesting Fact: In the past, "Glenlivet" has been used as a name for the 'terroir' in the Speyside region, resulting in some older bottles of recognized brands (circa 1970s and 1980s) including the Glenlivet name (e.g. Macallan-Glenlivet, Balvenie-Glenlivet).

Highland Park

Region: Highland (Island)

Founded: 1798

Interesting Fact: Highland Park's distillery is the northernmost distillery in Scotland, as one of two distilleries on the island of Orkney.

Arran

Region: Highland (Island)

Founded: 1995

Interesting Fact: During the construction of the distillery in 1994, a pair of golden eagles built their nest on a cliff near the distillery; as golden eagles are a protected species, this temporarily halted construction of the distillery.

Lagavulin

Region: Islay

Founded: 1816

Interesting Fact: Lagavulin 16-year is the preferred scotch of Ron Swanson, a popular character from the TV series Parks & Recreation.

Laphroaig

Region: Islay

Founded: 1815

Interesting Fact: Laphroaig is the only Scotch whisky to carry the Royal Warrant of the Prince of Wales.

Macallan

Region: Speyside

Founded: 1824

Interesting Fact: The Macallan estate is rumored to be haunted.

Talisker

Region: Highland (Island)

Founded: 1830

Interesting Fact: In his poem "The Scotsman's Return From Abroad", Robert Louis Stevenson mentioned "The king o' drinks, as I conceive it, Talisker, Islay, or Glenlivet.".
